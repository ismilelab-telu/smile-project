Machine learning is easier to understand when its position inside AI is clear. Many terms in this area sound similar, but they actually sit at different levels.

Artificial Intelligence, or AI, is the broad umbrella for systems that perform tasks that usually require human intelligence. Examples include recognizing patterns, making decisions, understanding language, planning actions, or helping solve problems.

Machine learning is one way to build AI. The difference is that ML does not only run fixed rules written by humans. An ML system adjusts its behavior based on data and examples it has learned from.

## Simple Taxonomy

- **Artificial Intelligence** is the broad umbrella for systems that try to imitate or assist human thinking abilities.
- **Machine Learning** is a branch of AI that learns from data to make predictions, recommendations, scores, or decisions.
- **Neural Network** is one family of ML models that uses layers of artificial neurons as a mathematical structure.
- **Deep Learning** uses neural networks with many layers, so it is often strong for complex data such as images, audio, and text.
- **Generative AI** focuses on creating new content such as text, images, audio, video, or code.

## How They Relate

Not all AI is machine learning. Rule-based systems can also be called AI if they are designed to solve certain intelligent tasks.

Not all machine learning is deep learning. Many classic ML models such as linear regression, decision trees, and random forests do not use deep neural networks.

Modern generative AI often uses deep learning, but its goal is different: producing new content. Meanwhile, many other ML models focus more on prediction, classification, recommendation, or risk detection.

The simple map is: AI is the broad umbrella, machine learning is one branch of it, neural networks are one family of ML models, deep learning is deeper neural networks, and generative AI is an AI area that produces content.
