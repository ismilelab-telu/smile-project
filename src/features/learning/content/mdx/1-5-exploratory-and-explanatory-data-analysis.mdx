Exploratory and explanatory analysis are both part of understanding data, but they serve different purposes.

Exploratory data analysis is where you look around. You inspect distributions, relationships, gaps, outliers, and surprises. Explanatory analysis is where you communicate selected findings clearly to someone else.

## Exploratory Analysis

Exploration is intentionally open-ended. You are trying to learn what the dataset contains and what questions deserve attention.

Typical exploratory checks include:

- distribution of the target;
- missing values by column;
- unusual or impossible values;
- relationship between features and target;
- category counts and rare categories;
- outliers that may be valid or may be data errors;
- time patterns, segments, or changes across groups.

Exploration helps you discover what to clean, what to investigate, and what risks might affect modeling.

## Explanatory Analysis

Explanatory analysis is more selective. You choose the findings that matter and present them with a clear message.

For example, after exploring many charts, you might explain only one important result: demand is consistently higher during evening shifts when promotion is active.

The goal is not to show every chart. The goal is to make a claim that the evidence actually supports.

## Avoid Overclaiming

Charts can support a claim, but they do not automatically prove causation. A chart might show that two variables move together, but that does not prove one causes the other.

For ML workflow, good analysis helps identify target behavior, useful feature relationships, data quality issues, and modeling risks. It keeps the team from training a model before understanding what the data is saying.
